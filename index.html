How to Use the Inequality Calculator

Type your inequality into the input field using standard math notation. You can use >, <, >=, <=, or the ≥ and ≤ symbols. Click Solve or press Enter to get your answer instantly.

Supported Inequality Types

Linear Inequalities: Single-variable inequalities like 2x + 3 > 7 or -4x ≤ 12. The solver applies inverse operations to isolate x, flipping the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative.

Compound Inequalities: Double-sided inequalities like -3 < 2x + 1 ≤ 9 combining two conditions with AND/OR logic.

Absolute Value Inequalities: Expressions like |x - 2| < 5 that split into two cases.

Quadratic Inequalities: Solve x² - 4 > 0 and similar polynomial inequalities with our factoring engine.

Understanding the Output

Your solution is displayed in three forms: as an inequality (x > 2), in interval notation ((2, ∞)), and in set-builder notation ({x | x > 2}). The number line graph visually shows the solution set with open or closed endpoints.

Common Examples

• 3x - 6 > 0 → x > 2 → (2, ∞)

• -2x + 4 ≤ 10 → x ≥ -3 → [-3, ∞)

• |x| < 4 → -4 < x < 4 → (-4, 4)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an inequality in math?
An inequality is a mathematical statement that compares two expressions using symbols like > (greater than), < (less than), ≥ (greater than or equal to), or ≤ (less than or equal to). Unlike equations, inequalities have a range of solutions rather than a single value.
When do you flip the inequality sign?
You must flip (reverse) the inequality sign when you multiply or divide both sides of the inequality by a negative number. For example, if -2x < 6, dividing by -2 gives x > -3 (sign flips).
What is interval notation?
Interval notation is a compact way to write a range of numbers. Parentheses ( ) mean the endpoint is NOT included (strict inequality); brackets [ ] mean the endpoint IS included. For example, x > 3 is written (3, ∞) and x ≤ 5 is (-∞, 5].
Is this calculator free to use?

Can it solve compound inequalities?
Yes. Switch to the "Compound" tab and enter double-sided inequalities like -1 < 3x + 2 ≤ 14. The calculator will solve all parts simultaneously and display the result in interval notation.
